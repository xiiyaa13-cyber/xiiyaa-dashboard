Big Picture

Major U.S. indexes closed lower Tuesday on tariff news but pared steep morning losses. President Trump announced new tariffs—Mexico 25%, Canada 25%, China 10%—then delayed Mexico tariffs for one month after a border security and fentanyl deal. The S&P 500 fell 0.75%, Nasdaq dropped 1.2%, and the Dow declined 0.27%. Defensive sectors (healthcare, staples) led; tech and consumer discretionary lagged. Ford and GM recouped losses after the Mexico delay. The VIX reached its highest level in a week before easing.
